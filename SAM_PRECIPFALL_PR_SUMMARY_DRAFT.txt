## Summary

Make SAM `PrecipFall` component-conservative by sedimenting rain, snow, and graupel separately with donor-limited component fluxes. The same limited component fluxes now drive both state updates and surface precipitation accumulation.

## BFB / model impact

BFB changes are expected for SAM `PrecipFall`.

Reason: rain, snow, and graupel now sediment with conservative component-wise donor-limited fluxes. The same limited component fluxes drive both state updates and surface precipitation accumulation.

Expected impacts:
- SAM precipitating species may change where the old total-qp update and post-update clipping were active.
- Surface rain/snow/graupel accumulation diagnostics may change.
- The new behavior closes component-wise and total column precipitation budgets.

## What this PR does not change

- The legacy reduced-flux substep rule is unchanged.
- The public `Precip` latent-proxy roundtrip residual is left as a separate follow-up.
- Cloud, local `Precip` source algebra, and `IceFall` are not broadened in this fix.

## Follow-up: public Precip latent-proxy closure

The scalar `sam_precip_cell_update` helper conserves the SAM latent proxy in the mixed-phase riming/accretion case. A small residual was observed only on the full public path:

`Copy_State_to_Micro -> Compute_Coefficients -> Precip -> Copy_Micro_to_State`.

That path includes conserved/primitive conversion, EOS reconstruction of temperature and pressure, moisture clipping, and plane-averaged coefficient rows. It is therefore tracked as a separate thermodynamic roundtrip closure follow-up, not part of this `PrecipFall` sedimentation conservation fix.
